Vegetation Density is a Sign of Climate Change

People normally look to temperature changes, glaciers, and ocean levels for signs of climate change
but there is something else that reacts much faster to effects of climate change...

For each year, vegetation density changes with the season

with the vegetation being the most dense in the summer, least dense in the winter,
and transitioning in spring and fall

In a perfect world, the vegetation density would stay relatively stable for every year
and there would be minimal difference in vegetation density

...but because of climate change affecting the seasons there is a visible difference in vegetation density between years.

During the summer months, the vegetation density has been volatile

because of droughts and elevated levels of CO2
